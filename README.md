Subject: I think this USB drive contains a puzzle

Hi,
I found something strange on a USB stick, and I’m sending you everything I managed to copy from it.
The person this belonged to is someone I know. He has always been a real computer freak — the kind of person who notices patterns where everyone else sees noise. He knows his way around systems, files, databases, compression tools, and strange technical details most people would ignore. He is also a sharp observer, very methodical, and astronomy has been one of his long-time hobbies.

That is why I don’t think this is random.

Next to the USB drive there was a sheet of paper. It did not look like an ordinary note. It looked more like a fragment of instructions. Near the top, two phrases were written in large letters:

MASTER, DETAIL, SQL

Below that, the page seemed to split everything into two layers:

The lower part appeared to refer to the earth, places, or possibly a map.
The upper part mentioned stars. I remember seeing descriptions of five stars. There was also a line about compression, and another note saying that the data was hidden in colors.

I am not sure whether the colors referred to flags, pixels, map colors, or something embedded inside an image. On the computer screen, there was an image of Slovenia, which makes me think the “ground” part might be connected to locations on the map.

I also remember seeing something that looked like an observation schedule — possibly something he had sent to friends. One note suggested that he had distributed the work evenly, as if different people were supposed to observe or process different parts of working assignement.

The strangest part was a clearly circled number:
50

Under it, he had written what looked like a loop program:
for i = 0 to 49
G = i mod 15
S = f(Xi)
And next to it, very carefully written, was this exact sequence:
f(Xi) = [5, 4, 13, 16, 21, 12, 9, 1, 2, 6,  36, 34, 31, 30, 40, 41, 44, 43, 35, 36,  53, 55, 57, 61, 64, 63, 58, 57, 54, 56,  66, 65, 67, 60, 70, 72, 75, 73, 75, 76,  83, 82, 84, 86, 84, 88, 90, 90, 95, 93]

Knowing him, I doubt these numbers are accidental.

I took the USB stick and copied the contents of the file to my phone. I’m sending it to you because I honestly have no idea what he was trying to do, but now I’m curious too.

From what I can piece together, the file, the note, the colors, the map, the stars, and the compression all seem to belong to the same system.

One more thing stood out, although I honestly have no idea what it means.
On the note, there was a separate line saying that the inverse system had not yet been incorporated.
It felt less like an instruction and more like a warning or unfinished reminder — as if the main system worked in one direction, but whatever was supposed to run backwards was still missing.

A password was also written on the paper: "VIGENERE".

If you can make anything out of this, please let me know.
Gregor
